IT Workflow, Ticket, and Automation Prompts

Built around your RCSC-style work: these help create clearer tickets, escalation notes, user-friendly replies, and internal tools without sounding overly technical.
Low-level explanation prompt
Explain this to me in low-level language like I am trying to understand it well enough to communicate it, not like I am an expert.

Topic/problem: [PASTE TOPIC]

Give me:
- What it means
- Why it probably happened
- What parts matter
- What parts I do not need to overthink
- What I can say to the requester
- A simple analogy
- A concise answer I can use in a ticket
Ticket closeout builder
Create a ticket closeout note for this work.

Issue reported: [ISSUE]
What I checked: [WHAT YOU CHECKED]
What I found: [FINDINGS]
What I did: [ACTION TAKEN]
Result: [RESULT]

Make it professional, clear, and not overly technical. Include that the ticket is being closed for now, and ask them to contact us again if anything else comes up.
Escalation summary prompt
Create an escalation summary I can send to another IT team.

Keep it clear and factual, not dramatic.

Include:
- User/requester
- Location
- Device/system affected
- Reported issue
- What I already checked
- What changed recently, if known
- Screenshots/logs available, if any
- What I need from the other team
- Business impact in plain language

Avoid sounding like I already know the root cause unless the evidence is solid.
Before/after troubleshooting log
Turn my notes into a clean troubleshooting log.

Raw notes:
[PASTE NOTES]

Format it as:
- Reported issue
- Location/user
- Steps taken
- Findings
- Fix applied
- Testing performed
- Current status
- Follow-up needed

Use simple language and avoid jargon unless needed.
Internal tool safety prompt
I want to create an internal IT tool for [TOOL IDEA].

Before building it, review it for safety and supportability:
- What could go wrong?
- What permissions does it need?
- What should be read-only?
- What should require confirmation?
- What should be logged?
- What should have rollback/undo?
- What should never be stored in plain text?
- What should be configurable?
- What should be tested before anyone else uses it?

Then create the tool with those protections built in.
User impact wording prompt
Rewrite this IT update so it is polite, low-level, and not too technical.

Message:
[PASTE MESSAGE]

Tone:
- Helpful
- Calm
- Professional
- Clear
- Not overly apologetic
- Not overly expert-sounding
- Avoid saying urgent unless it truly is
- Give the person a clear next step if needed
Meeting prep prompt
Help me prepare for an IT support/project meeting.

Context:
[PASTE CONTEXT]

Give me:
- Main topics to bring up
- Questions I should ask
- Risks or blockers
- Things I should clarify
- Quick wins
- What I should avoid overcommitting to
- A simple agenda
- A short summary I can say out loud
Change plan prompt
Create a simple change plan for this IT task: [TASK].

Include:
- Goal
- Systems affected
- People affected
- Before-change checklist
- Step-by-step plan
- Backout/rollback plan
- Testing checklist
- Communication before and after
- What to document when done

Keep it practical for internal IT support.
Use these as strong constraints, checklists, and acceptance criteria. No prompt can override safety rules, tool limits, or unavailable information, but these are designed to remove ambiguity and make the output much more likely to match what you intended.
